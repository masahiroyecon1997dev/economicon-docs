## How to Treat the Results

- Economicon is a tool that helps with analysis. It does not automatically guarantee that the output should be treated as a final conclusion.
- Coefficients, standard errors, p-values, and confidence intervals depend on the data, model settings, preprocessing choices, and sample construction.
- Decisions about outliers, variable definitions, fixed effects, standard-error specifications, and sample restrictions can materially change interpretation.

## For Research, Reports, and Official Outputs

If you use Economicon output in theses, academic papers, conference materials, reports, or other official outputs, a double-check with a separate analytical method is recommended.

1. Recheck the original data and all preprocessing steps.
2. Confirm the assumptions of the chosen estimator against textbooks, papers, lecture notes, or other reliable references.
3. Verify the main results with another tool, an independent recalculation, a manual spot check, or review by a coauthor or supervisor.
4. Record the version, settings, variable definitions, and sample conditions so the analysis remains reproducible.

:::caution[Important]
For submissions, publications, conference materials, and other externally shared results, it is best to confirm the main findings with at least one separate analytical route before treating them as final.
:::
